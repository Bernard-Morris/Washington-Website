Washington State Information

Washington State is renowned in the Pacific Northwest for its diverse landscapes, dynamic cities, and flourishing industries. The state offers a fantastic natural wonder, from the majestic peaks of the Cascade Mountains to the vast temperate rainforests of the Olympic Peninsula. Its cities, including Seattle, Tacoma, and Olympia, are hubs of culture, innovation, and historical significance. Furthermore, Washington holds a prominent position in technology, aerospace, and agriculture, establishing itself as a crucial contributor to the national economy. Whether exploring its awe-inspiring parks or enjoying its art, music, sports, and science, along with the urban-to-rural atmosphere, Washington State provides everyone with abundant opportunities.

Kennewick Man

Along the Columbia River, a 9,300-year-old skeleton known as the Kennewick Man was discovered in Kennewick, WA. [4] [5]

Hydropower

Grand Coulee Dam is the biggest hydropower producer in the United States. The dam produces power to 2 million households each year. [9a] [10]

Deep Waters

Lake Chelan, at 1,486ft(435m), is the third deepest lake in the country. Its lowest point is 387ft(118m) below sea level. [9b] [17]
